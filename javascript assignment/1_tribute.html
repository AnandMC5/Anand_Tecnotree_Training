Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda (1863-1902) was an Indian Hindu monk and a key figure in the introduction of Indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the Western world. He was a disciple of the Indian mystic Ramakrishna and founded the Ramakrishna Math and the Ramakrishna Mission to propagate the teachings of his guru.

"Arise, awake, and stop not until the goal is reached."

Swami Vivekananda's famous address at the Parliament of the World's Religions in Chicago in 1893 marked the beginning of the spread of Hinduism and Yoga in the West. His teachings on spiritual unity and the harmony of religions continue to inspire millions of people around the world.

In his short life of 39 years, Swami Vivekananda left a lasting legacy that continues to impact the lives of millions of people. He was a true spiritual leader who dedicated his life to the upliftment of humanity.
